What are our features?

The unique methods of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) are reflected in various aspects of diagnosis and health management. Here are some core concepts and practices:

    1. Holistic Concept: TCM views the human body as an organic whole where the organs and systems are interconnected and influence each other. It also considers the human body in unity with the natural and social environment.
    1. Syndrome Differentiation and Treatment: The core principle of TCM treatment is to make a personalized diagnosis based on the patient’s specific symptoms and signs, and then determine the treatment plan according to the syndrome differentiation.
    1. Yin-Yang and Five Elements: The theories of Yin-Yang and Five Elements in TCM are important foundational theories for explaining life phenomena and guiding clinical practice.
    1. Pulse Diagnosis: Obtaining information about a patient’s health status by feeling the pulse is one of the unique diagnostic methods in TCM.
    1. Use of Herbal Medicine: Treating diseases with natural herbs, where TCM formulas typically include multiple ingredients aimed at harmonizing Yin and Yang, nourishing Qi and blood, and unblocking meridians.
    1. Acupuncture and Moxibustion: Regulating Qi and blood, and promoting the body’s self-healing ability by inserting needles or applying moxibustion at specific acupoints.
    1. Tuina (Massage) and Manipulation: Using manual techniques to relax muscles, regulate Qi and blood, and improve organ function.
    1. Diet Therapy: Regulating the body and preventing or treating diseases by using the properties of food according to its temperature, flavor, and meridian tropism.
    1. Emotional Regulation: TCM believes that emotional states significantly affect health, so it advocates adjusting emotions to maintain well-being.
    1. Health Preservation: Emphasizing prevention, TCM promotes maintaining and enhancing health through daily lifestyle, diet, exercise, and other practices.
    1. Meridian Theory: TCM posits that meridians are channels for the flow of Qi and blood, and regulating meridians can influence organ functions.
    1. Cupping and Gua Sha: Unblocking meridians, activating blood circulation, and relieving muscle tension through cupping and Gua Sha techniques.
    1. Seasonal Health Preservation: Adjusting lifestyle and diet according to the changes of the four seasons to align with natural cycles.
    1. Music Therapy: Using music to regulate emotions and physical states for therapeutic and health preservation purposes.
    1. Qigong: Cultivating and regulating the body’s Qi through practices of breath control, physical posture, and meditation.

These methods form the unique treatment and health management system of TCM, aiming to achieve comprehensive health in physical, mental, and social adaptation capabilities.

1 - Acupuncture treatment!

Compatible with auxiliary treatment for a variety of diseases and pain

What is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is part of Traditional Chinese Medicine practice that entails stimulating specific points on the body, most often with a needle penetrating the skin.

What are some conditions that are suitable for acupuncture treatment?

acupuncture is applicable for a wide range of conditions, including but not limited to the following categories:

  • 1.Neurological Diseases: Such as post-stroke sequelae, migraines, trigeminal neuralgia, sciatica, peripheral facial palsy, neurasthenia, hysteria, epilepsy, intercostal neuralgia, schizophrenia, traumatic paraplegia, brachial plexus neuralgia, peripheral nerve injury, headache, etc.

  • 2.Respiratory System Diseases: Including bronchial asthma, acute and chronic bronchitis, upper respiratory tract infections, acute tonsillitis, etc.

  • 3.Digestive System Diseases: Such as gastric ptosis, peptic ulcers, diarrhea, acute and chronic gastritis, neurogenic vomiting, constipation, gastrointestinal neurosis, diaphragmatic spasm, hyperacidity, acute gastroenteritis, chronic enteritis, pyloric spasm, bacterial dysentery, etc.

  • 4.Gynecological Diseases: Dysmenorrhea, amenorrhea, functional uterine bleeding, uterine prolapse, uterine fibroids, infertility, pelvic inflammatory disease, etc.

  • 5.Cardiovascular System Diseases: Such as hypertension, coronary heart disease, angina pectoris, arrhythmias, cardiac neurosis, etc.

  • 6.Dermatological Diseases: Urticaria, herpes zoster, neurodermatitis, etc.

  • 7.Endocrine System Diseases: Such as simple goiter, diabetes, hyperthyroidism, etc.

  • 8.Musculoskeletal and Connective Tissue Diseases: Cervical spondylosis, rheumatoid arthritis, periarthritis of the shoulder, lumbar pain, rheumatic arthritis, osteoarthritis, acute lumbar sprain, etc.

  • 9.Otorhinolaryngological Diseases: Chronic rhinitis, pharyngitis, conjunctivitis, epistaxis, toothache, neurogenic deafness, neurogenic tinnitus, Meniere’s disease, etc.

  • 10.Urological and Reproductive System Diseases: Such as impotence, prostatitis, chronic nephritis, etc.

  • 11.Pediatric Diseases: Such as enuresis, poliomyelitis sequelae, etc.

  • 12.Surgical Diseases: Such as cholecystolithiasis, intestinal obstruction, etc.

In addition, acupuncture is also used to treat acute and chronic pain, such as neck, shoulder, waist, and leg pain, headache, dysmenorrhea, etc., as well as some functional diseases and chronic diseases. The scope of indications for acupuncture treatment is very broad, but it should be performed by qualified personnel to ensure safety and therapeutic effects.

2 - Acupressure (Tuina)

In addition to professional massage, it also includes other Trerapy Combo (Cupping,Guasha,Hot Stone,Facial)

Acupressure uses fingers, palms or elbows, or with various devices to clear blockages in body meridians. Acupressure is commonly used for treating specific musculoskeletal disorders. It is also effective in chronic stress-related disorders of the digestive, respiratory, and reproductive systems.

What are the conditions that are suitable for Tuina treatment?

Acupressure（Tuina）, also known as “massage” in English, is a traditional Chinese medical treatment that involves applying various manual techniques to stimulate the skin, muscles, joints, nerves, blood vessels, and lymphatic system of the body. This practice promotes local blood circulation, improves metabolism, and enhances the body’s natural disease resistance, promoting the absorption of inflammatory exudates, and alleviating muscle spasms and pain. Tuina can relax muscles and tendons, activate blood circulation, disperse blood stasis, reduce swelling, and relieve pain. It is therefore suitable for treating a variety of conditions, including but not limited to the following categories:

    1. Orthopedic Conditions: Including cervical spondylosis, periarthritis of the shoulder, lumbar disc herniation, osteoarthritis of the knee joint, joint adhesion, stiffness, and soft tissue contracture after fracture healing, etc.
    1. Soft Tissue Injuries: Such as sprains of major joints and muscles throughout the body, muscle strains, and muscle fatigue.
    1. Neurological Conditions: Including migraines, trigeminal neuralgia, sciatica, etc.
    1. Digestive System Conditions: Such as stomach pain, indigestion, gastric ptosis, chronic gastritis, etc.
    1. Gynecological Conditions: Including dysmenorrhea, menstrual irregularities, menopausal syndrome, etc.
    1. Pediatric Conditions: Such as fever in children, diarrhea, muscular torticollis, food accumulation, colds, cough, etc.
    1. Endocrine and Functional Disorders: Such as diabetes, hypertension, etc.
    1. Beauty and Weight Loss: Tuina can also be used for beauty and weight loss treatments.
    1. Health Preservation: Tuina can also be used for leisure relaxation and health preservation.

It is important to note that while tuina has a broad range of therapeutic applications, there are also some contraindications, such as various acute infectious diseases, acute osteomyelitis, tuberculous arthritis, infectious skin diseases, skin eczema, burns from fire and water, skin ulcers, tumors, and various sores. In addition, women during menstruation, pregnant women after five months of pregnancy, patients with acute peritonitis, acute purulent peritonitis, acute appendicitis, and those who are chronically very weak, have serious cardiovascular diseases, or are elderly and frail, are contraindicated for massage. Before choosing tuina treatment, a clear diagnosis should be made, and it should only be carried out for those who are confirmed to be suitable.

3 - Cupping Therapy!

Adapt to a variety of diseases and improve immunity

Cupping therapy is an ancient form of alternative medicine in which a therapist puts special cups on your skin for a few minutes to create suction. People get it for many purposes, including to help with pain, inflammation, blood flow, relaxation and well-being, and as a type of deep-tissue massage.

What conditions is cupping therapy suitable for?

Cupping therapy is a traditional Chinese medical treatment that promotes blood circulation, eliminates blood stasis, and regulates the flow of qi and blood by creating a negative pressure on the skin. cupping therapy is suitable for a variety of conditions, including but not limited to the following categories:

    1. Musculoskeletal System Diseases: Such as shoulder and back pain, leg pain, cervical spondylosis, periarthritis of the shoulder, lumbar disc herniation, knee joint pain, etc.
    1. Respiratory System Diseases: Acute and chronic bronchitis, asthma, pneumonia, etc.
    1. Digestive System Diseases: Stomach pain, indigestion, chronic gastritis, etc.
    1. Neurological Diseases: Headaches, neuralgia, occipital neuralgia, intercostal neuralgia, sciatica, etc.
    1. Gynecological Conditions: Dysmenorrhea, menstrual irregularities, amenorrhea, postpartum lactation deficiency, etc.
    1. Pediatric Diseases: Pediatric fever, diarrhea in children, enuresis in children, etc.
    1. Skin Diseases: Herpes zoster, skin itching, urticaria, acne, etc.
    1. Other Diseases: Common cold with wind-cold, cough, asthma, hypertension, diabetes, etc.

Contraindications for cupping therapy include： acute severe diseases, contact infectious diseases, severe heart disease, heart failure, skin allergies, infectious skin diseases, skin tumors, skin ulcers, bleeding disorders, acute traumatic fractures, moderate and severe edema, schizophrenia, convulsions, high levels of neurosis, and non-cooperative individuals.

It is important to note that while cupping therapy has a wide range of applications, it is not suitable for all diseases and has some contraindications. Before undergoing cupping treatment, it should be assessed by a professional Chinese medicine practitioner to determine whether cupping therapy is appropriate.
